How does the Gini coefficient relate to the income share of the bottom and top fifth quintile?

The gini coefficient is a is a measure of statistical dispersion intended to represent the income or wealth distribution of a nation's residents, and is the most commonly used measure of inequality. The value of q1 and q5 (the first and fifth quintile) represents the share of total income going to the poorest and wealthiest 25% of the population. For example, if q5 is 50%, that means that 50% of total income is earned by the top 1/5th wealthiest households.
